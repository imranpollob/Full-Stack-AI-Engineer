# Week 9: Neural Networks and Deep Learning Fundamentals

Welcome to Week 9 of the Full-Stack AI Engineer bootcamp! For the past 8 weeks, we have mastered "Classical Machine Learning". We used algorithms like Random Forests and XGBoost on tabular data.

But what happens when your data isn't a spreadsheet? What happens if your data is a photograph of a tumor? Or an audio recording of a spoken sentence? Or the entire text of a Shakespearean play?

Classical Machine Learning completely fails on unstructured data. To solve these problems, we must mimic the architecture of the human brain. Welcome to the world of **Deep Learning**.

## What We'll Cover This Week

*   **Day 1: Intro to Neural Networks.** We move from Scikit-Learn to `TensorFlow` and `PyTorch`. We learn how artificial "Neurons" connect together to form massive decision-making matrixes.
*   **Day 2: Forward Propagation & Activation Functions.** We learn the math of how data flows *forward* through a network. We introduce Activation functions like `ReLU` and `Softmax` that bend straight lines into curves.
*   **Day 3: Loss Functions & Backpropagation.** How does a network "learn"? We introduce the algorithm that made Deep Learning possible: calculating mathematical errors and propagating them *backward* through the layers using Calculus.
*   **Day 4: Gradient Descent Optimizers.** Backpropagation finds the mistake, but Gradient Descent fixes it. We learn how algorithms like `SGD` and `Adam` mathematically surf down the error curve.
*   **Day 5: Building Networks in TensorFlow/Keras.** We write our first actual Deep Learning code using Google's production framework to classify handwritten digits out of the famous MNIST dataset.
*   **Day 6: Building Networks in PyTorch.** We rewrite the exact same digit-classifier using Meta's `PyTorch`—the undisputed king of modern AI research and Large Language Models.
*   **Day 7: The Deep Learning Capstone.** We scale up! We graduate from black-and-white numbers to the CIFAR-10 dataset (Full-color photos of airplanes, horses, and dogs) and train a massive Neural Network to classify them.

## Why This Matters
Deep Learning powers ChatGPT, Tesla Autopilot, and modern facial recognition. It is the single most important technology of the 21st century. 

Unlike Random Forests, Neural Networks automatically perform their own Feature Engineering! They independently discover edges, textures, and concepts directly from raw pixels.
